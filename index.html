The Legend & Legacy of the Nine-Eyed Dzi

From the misty highlands of Tibet comes a relic steeped in mystery, the Nine-Eyed Dzi. Some say it was a divine gift that fell from the heavens; others whisper it was crafted by the Serpent Kings, rulers of ancient subterranean realms. Nine eyes, each a portal of fortune, power, and spiritual clarity.

Symbolism & Power

The nine eyes represent the alignment of blessings: compassion, glory, protection, health, wealth, longevity, success, wisdom, and the clearing of karmic obstacles.

  • 1 Eye: Hope, wisdom, happiness
  • 2 Eyes: Harmony, balance of Yin & Yang
  • 3 Eyes: Luck, honor, longevity
  • 4 Eyes: Overcomes negative energies
  • 5 Eyes: Wish-fulfilling bead
  • 6 Eyes: Emotional balance
  • 7 Eyes: Health, wealth, relationships, longevity
  • 8 Eyes: Disaster protection
  • 9 Eyes: Prosperity, protection from harm

Modern Stories & Mystique — The Nagoya Survivor’s Bead

In the 1990s, a China Airlines jet tragically crashed in Nagoya, Japan. Amid the devastation, one passenger emerged almost unharmed, wearing a single Dzi bead. Collectors still speak of this as living proof of the relic’s mystical protection.

Why So Few People Know About Dzi Beads

Unlike diamonds or gold, the Dzi was never meant for the masses. It remained a secret among Himalayan highlanders and a handful of collectors, mystics, and monks.
